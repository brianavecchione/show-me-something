Briana's Birthday Open Call, Part II:
"Show Me Something That Changed Your Life"

Ten years ago, I ran a crowdsourced experiment for my birthday. The experiment was simple: an open call asking everyone in my network to share something that changed their life.

I published a separate essay on the experience, but the short version is that I came away with so many “gifts” from others that have stuck with me ever since. Crowdsourcing stories and artifacts was invaluable beyond my expectations, and not only because I learned more about the inner worlds of people I already knew (or, in some cases, people whose names I didn’t recognize).

This year, I'm reopening the call.

In my early twenties, one of the things the experiment taught me was how seemingly unrelated things—like a work of art, a personal anecdote, a relationship, or a care practice—converge on the full range of human experience. Love, gratitude, wonder, forgiveness, empathy, grief, belonging, resilience, hope, joy, courage, reverence. The things that change one’s life are multifaceted, and that itself is often necessary for depth to form and meaning to arise.

The form takes about five minutes. There's one central question: “Show me something that has changed your life”, plus a couple of privacy fields. Responses are private, and the form itself is end-to-end encrypted.

At the end of the month, I'll release the responses publicly for those who've consented. Names are anonymized by default, but you can choose to include yours, or to omit anything you'd rather not share broadly.
