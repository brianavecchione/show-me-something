# Briana's Birthday Open Call, Part II: Show Me Something That Changed Your Life

Ten years ago, I ran a crowdsourced experiment for my birthday. The experiment was simple: an open call asking everyone in my network to share something that changed their life.

I published a [separate essay](https://substack.com/@brianav1/p-151751824) on this experience, but the short version is that I came away with so many "gifts" from others that have stuck with me ever since. Crowdsourcing stories and artifacts was invaluable beyond my expectations, and not only because I learned more about the inner worlds of people I already knew (or, in some cases, people whose names I didn't recognize).

This year, **I'm reopening the call**.

In my early twenties, one of the things I took from the experience was how seemingly unrelated things — like a work of art, personal experience, relationship, or a care practice — converge on the full range of human experience. Love, gratitude, wonder, forgiveness, empathy, grief, belonging, resilience, hope, joy, courage, reverence. Often, the things that change one's life can be simultaneously difficult and fulfilling. And sometimes that's a necessary prerequisite for ascribing depth and meaning to them.

The form takes about five minutes. There's one central question: "Show me something that has changed your life", plus a couple of privacy fields. Responses are private, and the form itself is end-to-end encrypted.

At the end of the month, I'll release the responses publicly for those who've consented. Names are anonymized by default, but you can choose to include yours, or to omit anything you'd rather not share broadly.

If you'd like to participate, you can do so [here.](https://dash.palform.app/fill/org_0PXS602PN0RZE/form_0PXS6K7VS0R2C?f=fat_0PXSB15FH0KCR#ak=A994AEB12ED16D518FD5B9C686CA76B092783879)

Thank you so much for your time and willingness to share! If you'd like to pass this along to others, please do. I'll follow up at the end of the month with the submissions, and hopefully, we can all be changed in some small way together.
